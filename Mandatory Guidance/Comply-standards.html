International Standards

1 Purpose, Authority & Responsibility Documentation

Determine if the purpose, authority, and responsibility of the internal audit activity are documented in audit charter, approved by the Board, and communicated to the engagement clients

2 Understanding & Application

Demonstrate an understanding of the purpose, authority, and responsibility of the internal audit activity

The Standards have already been discussed above, so we will not discuss them again here from an overall perspective. In the syllabus there are seven subtopics that we look at in the material.

This topic has been largely addressed within Definition of Internal Auditing, so we will not duplicate the discussion of the Charter and the discussion of the purpose, authority, and responsibility of the internal audit activity.

Key Point: In addition to the IIA understanding these issues, it is critical that they also communicate these things to engagement clients. By communicating this information about what the IIA does and does not do, there will be a reduction in the number of misunderstandings between the IAA and the engagement clients. This will enable to IIA to better fulfill its duties and responsibilities.
