What is Mandatory Guidance?

Mandatory guidance refers to standards and principles from the IIA that must be followed. The International Professional Practices Framework (IPPF) provides structure for internal auditing guidance.

Three Sources of Mandatory Guidance

Element Purpose
Definition of Internal Auditing States the fundamental purpose, nature, and scope of internal auditing
Code of Ethics States principles and behavioral expectations for internal auditors
International Standards Provides framework for performing and promoting internal auditing

Types of Standards

Attribute Standards (1000-1300)

Characteristics of organizations and individuals performing internal auditing

Performance Standards (2000-2600)

Nature of internal auditing and quality criteria for measuring performance

Implementation Standards

Specific requirements for Assurance (A) or Consulting (C) activities

Key Points

  • Look first in Mandatory Guidance for answers
  • If no answer found, check Strongly Recommended Guidance
  • Standards use "must" for requirements and "should" for expected practices
  • Both Assurance and Consulting services must follow these standards
