IIA Code of Ethics

Abide by and promote compliance with The IIA Code of Ethics. The Code establishes trust and provides the foundation for ethical conduct in internal auditing. Memorize the four principles - they appear frequently on the exam.

Questions (3)

The Four Principles

Principle Definition
1. Integrity Establishes trust and provides the basis for reliance on their judgment
2. Objectivity Exhibit professional objectivity in gathering, evaluating, and communicating information
3. Confidentiality Respect the value and ownership of information and do not disclose without authority
4. Competency Apply the knowledge, skills, and experience needed to perform services effectively

Rules of Conduct

1. Integrity

  • Perform work with honesty, diligence, and responsibility
  • Observe the law and make required disclosures
  • Avoid illegal activities and acts discreditable to the profession
  • Support legitimate organizational objectives

2. Objectivity

  • Avoid activities that may impair unbiased assessment
  • Don't accept gifts that could impair professional judgment
  • Disclose all material facts that could distort reporting

3. Confidentiality

  • Protect information acquired during duties
  • Never use information for personal gain

4. Competency

  • Only take on work you're qualified to perform
  • Perform services in accordance with International Standards
  • Continually improve your skills and service quality

Key Exam Points

  • The Code applies to all internal auditors and organizations
  • Breaches can result in disciplinary action
  • Focus on applying these principles to different scenarios
  • Remember: These build trust and credibility for the profession
