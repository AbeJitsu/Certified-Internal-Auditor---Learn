CIA Part 1 Exam Overview

The CIA Part 1 exam, Internal Audit Basics, is 150 minutes (2 hours and 30 minutes) long and consists of 125 multiple-choice questions. This exam tests aspects of mandatory guidance from the International Professional Practices Framework (IPPF), internal control and risk concepts, and tools and techniques for conducting internal audit engagements.

For more information about the exams, visit the IIA's website: www.iia.org

Exam Sections Overview

There are three Sections in the Part 1 Exam syllabus. These Sections and the percentages of the exam that these Sections represent are:

35–45%

Chapter 1 (Section I)

Mandatory Guidance

25–35%

Chapter 2 (Section II)

Internal Control and Risk

25–35%

Chapter 3 (Section III)

Conducting Internal Audit Engagements

Knowledge Levels Required

Additionally, the IIA syllabus gives guidance for the depth to which you must know the topics. There are two levels:

PROFICIENCY

Candidates must exhibit proficiency (thorough understanding and ability to apply concepts)

AWARENESS

Candidates must exhibit awareness (knowledge of terminology and fundamentals)

Sections I & III
are tested at the Proficiency Level

and

Section II
is tested at the Awareness Level

Exam Preparation Strategy

In preparing for the exam, you should read the textbook and use the study bank and practice exams to review questions from past exams. Many of the exam topics are very large and by studying past exam questions you can get a feeling for the manner and depth to which a topic has been tested.
