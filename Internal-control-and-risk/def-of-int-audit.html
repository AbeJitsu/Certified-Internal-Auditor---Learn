Definition of Internal Auditing

Internal auditing is an independent, objective assurance and consulting activity designed to add value and improve an organization's operations. It helps an organization accomplish its objectives by bringing a systematic, disciplined approach to evaluate and improve the effectiveness of risk management, control, and governance processes.

Standard 1000 - Purpose, Authority, and Responsibility

The purpose, authority, and responsibility of the internal audit activity must be formally defined in an internal audit charter, consistent with the Definition of Internal Auditing, Code of Ethics, and Standards.

The Internal Audit Charter Must Include:

Element Description
Scope Services and work to be performed
Authority Access to records, personnel, and physical properties
Objectives Goals of the internal audit activity
Responsibility & Accountability Duties and reporting requirements

Organizational Structure

Reporting Lines

CAE reports functionally to Board/Audit Committee; administratively to CEO

Independence

Must be independent of functions being audited

Organizational Status

High enough organizational level to be effective

The Audit Committee

A subcommittee of the board of directors composed of independent non-executive directors. Oversees both internal and external audit activities.

Key Responsibilities:

  • Ensure external auditor independence
  • Review financial communications and statements
  • Oversee internal audit strategy and resources
  • Review risk management and control evaluations
  • Appoint and compensate external auditors
  • Mediate between management and auditors

Internal Auditor Responsibilities

  • Test compliance with established controls and procedures
  • Evaluate adequacy and effectiveness of control systems
  • Examine reliability and integrity of financial and operating information
  • Review compliance with policies, procedures, and regulations
  • Evaluate effective and efficient use of resources
  • Provide analyses, recommendations, and counsel to management

Consulting Services

Internal audit may provide consulting services (advisory, counsel, facilitation) if defined in the charter. Must maintain objectivity and not assume management responsibility. Both assurance and consulting services add value to the organization.
